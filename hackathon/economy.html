What is a Circular Economy




A circular economy is an economic economy whose desire is aimed at eliminating waste and pollution by the continual use of resources.




Reusing Plastic Waste

In 2020, Canada produced nearly 3.3 million tonnes of plastic waste in a single calendar year. Of this total, 2.8 million tonnes was sent straight to a landfill, which is equivalent to nearly 25 CN Towers. This is bad. We can only keep destroying our planet for so long before we need change. But what if we told you there's still time to do so. At 3D BioWaste, our aim at the moment is to take huge amounts of waste from large companies and help the human race with it thru the use of prosthetics.




Plastic Waste for Healthcare

By reusing plastic waste as filament for 3D printers, we will be able to create a lot more prosthetics for much cheaper, benefitting patients who drastically need this new change. For example, using traditional techniques to build a prosthetic leg perhaps, could costs upwards from $5,000 to nearly $50,000! That's incredibly high for a basic human need, and by reusing waste for healthcare purposes, we wish to provide a much cheaper solution to this issue.




3D Printing Technology

By using 3D printing technology, we are producing minimal waste as well as producing things much faster than humans ever could. The thing about 3D printers is that since 3D printers aren’t like humans, they don’t need to take breaks periodically, instead, their working hours are far more versatile and they are a lot more efficient than humans are. This will only help in the healthcare industry in the longterm, and who knows how far we'll be able to take this.
